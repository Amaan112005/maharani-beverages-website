Decoding the Legal Requirements of Purity

In India, the manufacturing of packaged drinking water is governed by stringent regulatory frameworks designed to protect public health. The two primary governing bodies are the FSSAI (Food Safety and Standards Authority of India) and the BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards). Understanding these standards is essential for consumers who value transparency and safety.

Section 01

The IS 14543 Mandate.

Every bottle of packaged drinking water in India must comply with the IS 14543 standard set by BIS. This standard specifies the microbiological, chemical, and physical parameters that the water must satisfy. Key requirements include:

Parameter Standard Requirement
TDS (Total Dissolved Solids) Maximum 500 mg/l
pH Value 6.5 to 8.5
Turbidity Maximum 2 NTU
Microbiological Contaminants Must be Nil (0)
Section 02

The Role of FSSAI Licensing.

While BIS governs the quality of the water, FSSAI governs the entire food business operation. An FSSAI license (like Zenith's License No. 12825999000938) confirms that:

  • The facility meets strict sanitary and hygienic requirements.
  • Processing equipment is food-grade and regularly maintained.
  • Staff follow strict hygiene protocols.
  • Records of batch testing are meticulously maintained.
Section 03

Why Certification Matters.

When you consume a brand that is both BIS and FSSAI certified, you are guaranteed that the water has undergone rigorous multi-stage purification—including UV treatment and Ozonation—to eliminate all biological and chemical risks.
