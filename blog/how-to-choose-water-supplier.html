HOW TO CHOOSE THE RIGHT
WATER SUPPLIER

A Framework for Corporate & Residential Hydration

Water is the most consumed commodity in any workspace or household, yet it is often the least scrutinized. Choosing a packaged drinking water supplier is not merely a logistical decision; it is a fundamental health and safety mandate. Whether you are managing a corporate office in Kolkata or a household in Howrah, the criteria for selection must go beyond price.

Criteria 01

Mandatory Certifications & Legal Compliance.

The first filter for any premium supplier is their legal standing. In India, packaged drinking water is strictly regulated. A reliable partner must possess:

  • FSSAI License: The primary food safety certification (e.g., Zenith's License No. 12825999000938).
  • BIS Certification (ISI Mark): Compliance with IS 14543 is mandatory for packaged drinking water.
  • ISO 9001:2015: Signifies a globally recognized quality management system.
Criteria 02

Filtration Technology & Purity Standards.

Not all "filtered" water is equal. A high-performance supplier should utilize a multi-stage purification process. Look for systems that include:

  • Reverse Osmosis (RO): To remove dissolved solids and heavy metals.
  • UV Sterilization: To eliminate microbiological threats.
  • Ozonation: To ensure the water remains sterile inside the bottle without preservatives.
  • Mineral Balancing: Adding back essential electrolytes for health and taste.

Criteria 03

Logistical Reliability & Distribution Depth.

Purity is irrelevant if delivery is inconsistent. When choosing a supplier in the Kolkata or Howrah region, evaluate their distribution infrastructure:

  • Do they offer a 24-48 hour delivery rotation?
  • Do they have a dedicated fleet for bulk water supply?
  • Is their source close to the distribution hub to ensure fresh stock?
